Pokemon Simulator

The motivation behind this project stemmed from my lifelong passion for Pokémon and a desire to create an engaging simulation experience that resonates with other enthusiasts. This project also provided an excellent opportunity to learn new programming techniques and enhance my coding skills. By developing this simulator, I aimed to solve the problem of finding a comprehensive and interactive Pokémon simulation that allows users to fully immerse themselves in the process of catching, training, and battling Pokémon.

The functional requirements of the Pokémon simulator are designed to ensure a rich and interactive user experience. The main functionalities include:

  • Pokémon Collection: Users can collect and view a comprehensive list of Pokémon they have captured or traded, allowing them to track their progress and build their collection.
  • Catching Pokémon: The simulator allows users to simulate the experience of catching Pokémon using items like Poké Balls. Different Pokémon present varying levels of difficulty to capture, adding to the challenge and excitement.
  • Training and Battling: Users can train their Pokémon and engage in battles against other trainers. This feature simulates the competitive aspect of Pokémon training and battling.
  • Evolution: Users can evolve their Pokémon, enhancing their abilities and unlocking new potential, just like in the original Pokémon games.

We designed this project to provide users with multiple ways to interact with the simulator, including the utilization of Pokémon, interactions between trainers, and the special attack and defense capabilities of each Pokémon.

In the implementation phase, my team's primary responsibility was to develop the core functionalities of the simulator, including the Pokémon collection, catching mechanics, and battle system. I employed test-driven development (TDD) to ensure that each feature was thoroughly tested and functional before moving on to the next. This approach allowed for a structured and efficient development process.

Additionally, I utilized agile methodologies to iteratively improve the simulator based on feedback and testing. This iterative approach ensured that the final product met the desired quality and functionality standards.
